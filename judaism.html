Recommended reading

Here is most of what I read before, during, and after converting. Books in bold are particularly recommended. List starts earliest (2014) to latest.

  • Lamentations.
  • Synagogue Boards: A Sacred Trust, by Daniel Schechter.
  • Israel, by Noa Tishby.
  • Jerusalem, by Guy Delisle.
  • Choosing a Jewish Life, by Anita Diamont.
  • Why I Am A Jew, by Edmond Fleg.
  • Words That Hurt, Words that Heal, by Joseph Telushkin.
  • God in Search of Man, by Abraham Joshua Heschel
  • Exodus and Revolution, by Michael Walzer
  • Pikei Avot.
  • The Seventh Million: The Israelis and the Holocaust, by Tom Segev.
  • Learn to Read Hebrew in 6 Weeks!, by Miiko Shaffier.
  • My Promised Land: The Triumph and Tragedy of Israel, by Ari Shavit
  • Judaism: A Very Short Introduction, by Norman Solomon.
  • Man’s Search for Meaning, by Victor E. Frankl
  • The Great Partnership: Science, Religion, and the Search for Meaning, by Rabbi Jonathan Sacks.
  • Letters to the Next Generation 2: Reflections on Jewish Life, by Rabbi Jonathan Sacks.
  • Surprised by God: How I Learned to Stop Worrying and Love Religion, by Danya Ruttenberg
  • Swimming in the Sea of Talmud: Lessons for Everyday Living, by Rabbi Michael Katz and Rabbi Gershon Schwartz.
  • The Second Mountain: The Quest for a Moral Life, by David Brooks
  • O Jerusalem, by Dominique Lapierre and Larry Collins.
  • To Life: A Celebration of Jewish Being and Thinking, by Harold Kushner
  • Living a Life that Matters, by Harold Kushner.
  • Essential Judaism: A Complete Guide to Beliefs, Customs and Rituals, by George Robinson.
  • How To Fight Anti-Semitism, by Bari Weiss.
  • Rise and Kill First, by Ronen Bergman.
  • Jewish Literacy: The Most Important Things to Know about the Jewish Religion, Its People, and Its History, by Joseph Telushkin.
  • Israel: A Concise History of a Nation Reborn, by Daniel Gordis.
  • The Case for Israel, by Alan Dershowitz.
  • Ben-Gurion: Father of Modern Israel, by Anita Shapira.
  • Schindler's List, by Thomas Keneally.
  • From Beirut to Jerusalem, by Thomas L. Friedman.
  • Israel/Palestine, by Alan Dowty.
  • Covering Islam: How the Media and the Experts Determine How We See the Rest of the World, by Edward W. Said
  • A History of Modern Iran, by Ervand Abrahamian.
  • Israel/Palestine: How to End the War of 1948 (Open Media Series), by Tanya Reinhart.
  • Palace Walk, by Naguib Mahfouz.
  • Persepolis 2, by Marjane Satrapi.
  • The Reluctant Fundamentalist, by Mohsin Hamid.
  • No god but God: The Origins, Evolution and Future of Islam, by Reza Aslan.
  • Empire of the Mind: A History of Iran, by Michael Axworthy.
  • The Media Relations Department of Hizbollah Wishes You a Happy Birthday: Unexpected Encounters in the Changing Middle East, by Neil MacFarquhar.
